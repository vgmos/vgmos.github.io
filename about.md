I am Vasu Gupta, an analog IC design engineer with a long-standing interest in circuits, computation, memory, and the kind of judgment that turns technical work into durable engineering.

My academic work moved through BITS Pilani, Technion, and Georgia Tech before settling into professional analog design. I use this site to keep the strongest parts of that path legible: what I worked on, what evidence exists, what I learned as a designer, and which ideas still shape how I think.

The writing is broader than a project list. Some notes are technical. Some are about craft, discipline, attention, faith, spirituality, relationships, and the inner work of becoming more honest while building a serious professional life. The aim is a public record with taste: useful enough for engineers, personal enough to be alive.

You can find me on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/vasugupta711) and [GitHub](https://github.com/vgmos).
