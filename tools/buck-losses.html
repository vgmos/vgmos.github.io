    This is an analytical intuition model at a typical 25 °C parameter corner, not a part-level signoff tool. Confirm a real design with manufacturer models, SPICE, thermal analysis, and measurement.

    Equations in the open

    V2 solves regulated volt-second balance over explicit high-side, dead-time, low-side, and zero-current intervals. Each interval carries exact ∫i dt and ∫i² dt moments.

    CCM uses two dead-time windows and excludes both from low-side channel conduction. Diode-emulation DCM ends low-side conduction at zero current. Atomic loss rows disclose their formula, Chapter 4 equation, printed page, PDF page, and parameter provenance.

    Input power is reconstructed as POUT + PLOSS; efficiency is undefined at exactly zero load because burst, PFM, and minimum-on-time behavior are controller-dependent and out of scope.

    Scope & caveats

    1. Fixed-frequency diode-emulation DCM and forced CCM comparison are modeled; PFM, burst, and minimum-on-time control are not.
    2. GaN and synthetic silicon templates use typical 25 °C values without electrothermal iteration.
    3. Catalog magnetics use RMS copper plus a characterized residual exactly once. Missing core or out-of-domain EOSS data produces a subtotal.
    4. Transition timing is either derived from disclosed gate-charge phases or entered as an effective-time approximation.
    5. Bootstrap, snubbers, PCB/package resistance, ringing, and full IC leakage remain outside scope.
