    This is a first-order intuition tool, not a part-level signoff model. EPC2090 values anchor the switch defaults; overlap, dead time, passives, layout, temperature, and controller behavior remain editable assumptions.

    Equations In The Open

    The model stays in CCM and uses ideal duty \(D = V_{OUT}/V_{IN}\) without duty correction.

    Inductor ripple and RMS terms:

    $$\Delta i_L = \frac{(V_{IN}-V_{OUT})D}{f_{SW}L},\quad i_{PK}=I_{OUT}+\frac{\Delta i_L}{2},\quad i_{VAL}=I_{OUT}-\frac{\Delta i_L}{2}$$ $$I_{L,RMS}^2 = I_{OUT}^2+\frac{\Delta i_L^2}{12},\quad I_{C,RMS}^2=\frac{\Delta i_L^2}{12}$$ $$I_{HS,RMS}^2=D I_{L,RMS}^2,\quad I_{LS,RMS}^2=(1-D)I_{L,RMS}^2$$

    Loss terms:

    $$P_{HS}=I_{HS,RMS}^2R_{DS,H},\quad P_{LS}=I_{LS,RMS}^2R_{DS,L},\quad P_{DCR}=I_{L,RMS}^2R_{DCR}$$ $$P_{ESR}=I_{C,RMS}^2R_{ESR},\quad P_G=(Q_{G,H}+Q_{G,L})V_{DRV}f_{SW},\quad P_{BIAS}=V_{BIAS}I_Q$$ $$P_{SW}=\frac{1}{2}V_{IN}f_{SW}(t_r\max(i_{VAL},0)+t_f\max(i_{PK},0))$$ $$P_{DT}=V_{SD} f_{SW}t_{DT}(\max(i_{VAL},0)+\max(i_{PK},0))$$ $$P_{EOSS}=E_{OSS}f_{SW},\quad P_{Qrr}=V_{IN}Q_{rr}f_{SW}$$

    Total loss is the sum of the terms above. Efficiency is \(\eta=P_{OUT}/(P_{OUT}+P_{LOSS})\) for positive output power.

    Caveats

    1. Left of the forced-CCM boundary the inductor current reverses each cycle. Many real converters enter diode emulation, pulse-skipping, or PFM instead.
    2. RDS(on) does not iterate with junction temperature. EPC2090's 5.2 mΩ default is its 25 °C maximum at 5 V gate drive and 16 A.
    3. Inductor DCR always uses full RMS current. Characterized parts add a Coilcraft-derived AC/core residual after subtracting ripple I²R already counted in DCR. The manual AC/core entry is added separately at every load point.
    4. Overlap is a lumped edge-time estimate. Real switching loss also depends on the driver, package integration, layout, capacitances, and ringing.
    5. EOSS defaults use the EPC2090 energy-related effective capacitance as a first-order estimate. The real COSS curve is nonlinear with drain voltage.
    6. Input-capacitor ESR loss and thermal feedback are not modeled.
    7. Numbers are rounded deliberately. Use this to rank mechanisms, then confirm the design with detailed simulation and measurement.
