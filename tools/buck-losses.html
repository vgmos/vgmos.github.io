Loss Breakdown

    Equations In The Open

    The model stays in CCM and uses ideal duty \(D = V_{OUT}/V_{IN}\) without duty correction.

    Inductor ripple and RMS terms:

    $$\Delta i_L = \frac{(V_{IN}-V_{OUT})D}{f_{SW}L},\quad i_{PK}=I_{OUT}+\frac{\Delta i_L}{2},\quad i_{VAL}=I_{OUT}-\frac{\Delta i_L}{2}$$ $$I_{L,RMS}^2 = I_{OUT}^2+\frac{\Delta i_L^2}{12},\quad I_{C,RMS}^2=\frac{\Delta i_L^2}{12}$$ $$I_{HS,RMS}^2=D I_{L,RMS}^2,\quad I_{LS,RMS}^2=(1-D)I_{L,RMS}^2$$

    Loss terms:

    $$P_{HS}=I_{HS,RMS}^2R_{DS,H},\quad P_{LS}=I_{LS,RMS}^2R_{DS,L},\quad P_{DCR}=I_{L,RMS}^2R_{DCR}$$ $$P_{ESR}=I_{C,RMS}^2R_{ESR},\quad P_G=(Q_{G,H}+Q_{G,L})V_{DRV}f_{SW},\quad P_{BIAS}=V_{BIAS}I_Q$$ $$P_{SW}=\frac{1}{2}V_{IN}f_{SW}(t_r\max(i_{VAL},0)+t_f\max(i_{PK},0))$$ $$P_{DT}=V_F f_{SW}t_{DT}(\max(i_{VAL},0)+\max(i_{PK},0))$$ $$P_{EOSS}=E_{OSS}f_{SW},\quad P_{Qrr}=V_{IN}Q_{rr}f_{SW}$$

    Total loss is the sum of the terms above. Efficiency is \(\eta=P_{OUT}/(P_{OUT}+P_{LOSS})\) for positive output power.

    Caveats

    1. Left of the shaded boundary the inductor current reverses each cycle. The model shows synchronous forced-CCM behavior there; many real converters switch to diode emulation, pulse-skipping, or PFM instead, and their light-load efficiency differs — usually for the better.
    2. RDS(on) is whatever you type. The model doesn't iterate junction temperature, and hot FETs are 30–50% more resistive than their 25 °C number — bracket that by hand if it matters.
    3. Inductor DCR is counted; core loss is not. At high ripple or high frequency, core loss can rival DCR loss — treat the inductor bar as a floor.
    4. Overlap loss uses one lumped edge time. Real switching loss lives in gate drive, layout, capacitances, and ringing — use the overlap knob to bracket fast and slow edges, not to claim a part's exact behavior.
    5. EOSS and Qrr default to zero. At high input voltage and high frequency they stop being optional — the 48 V preset is a good place to try them.
    6. Input-capacitor ESR loss isn't modeled yet; in a buck it usually exceeds the output-capacitor term shown here.
    7. Numbers are rounded on purpose. The model ranks mechanisms and shows tradeoffs; it does not predict a datasheet curve to decimal places.
    8. Not a part selector, thermal simulator, EMI model, or SPICE replacement — it's the sketch you do before you open those.
