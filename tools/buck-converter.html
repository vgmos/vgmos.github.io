Steady-state Operation

Across the ON-time tON, the inductor sees vIN − vOUT and its current ramps up; across the OFF-time tOFF, it sees −vOUT and de-energizes into the output. Volt-second balance sets the duty cycle: D = tON/tSW = vOUT/vIN.

The peak-to-peak inductor-current ripple is ΔiL = tON · (vIN − vOUT)/LX, or equivalently ΔiL = (vIN − vOUT) · D/(fSW · LX).

ΔiL/iO of roughly 25–50 % is a reasonable starting point.

When DCM is enabled, the tool first checks the CCM valley current against the selected zero-cross threshold.

This model assumes no conduction or switching losses, no inductor DCR or core loss, no capacitor ESL, no dead time, and instantaneous switching. The optional CO ESR box is a first-order terminal-ripple term only: it reshapes vOUT by adding iC · RESR, but does not change duty cycle, current ripple, or efficiency. Efficiency is exactly 1 here, so D is also the ideal conversion ratio. A real converter runs slightly higher duty to cover its losses. To see where those losses actually go — conduction, switching, gate drive, dead time, DCR, ESR, and bias — open the Buck Converter Loss Tool.
