This site is a working notebook for engineering judgment: project writeups, technical assumptions, evidence, and the lessons that still shape how I work.

A good project page should say what problem was worth caring about, what I actually touched, which claims are supported, and what the work taught me about design.

The writing can widen from circuits into craft, attention, discipline, and the habits that make work clearer. That range matters to me, but the public standard stays the same: be useful, be precise, and avoid pretending certainty where the evidence is thin.

The aim is not to turn every source file into a page. It is to make the strongest work legible and to keep writing in a voice that can be both professional and alive.
