This site is being rebuilt as a quieter kind of archive.

For a long time, I imagined a project page as something mostly technical: diagrams, links, implementation notes, a clean list of things I had built or studied. That still matters. Older work from BITS Pilani, Technion, and Georgia Tech deserves a better record than scattered folders and fading context.

But the site I want now is broader than a technical archive. It should also hold the writing that comes after the formal research chapter: notes on engineering judgment, discipline, spiritual life, ambition, attention, and the strange work of becoming more honest with oneself.

The first pass here is simple. Make the space clean. Restore the old work carefully. Write in public with enough restraint that the page can stay professional, and enough honesty that it can stay alive.
